# MDX Network — Presentation Outline

## The Problem

- Social isolation is one of the biggest challenges students face — many struggle to meet people beyond their immediate course or friend group
- That isolation affects mental health, confidence, and motivation
- At the same time, students rarely get to work on real, meaningful projects with people from different backgrounds before entering the workforce
- There's no single place to find collaborators, discover what's happening, or have a say in campus life

## The Idea

MDX Network is a platform that connects students through real collaboration and shared experiences — reducing isolation by giving people a reason and a way to meet each other.

### 1. Collaborations — Meet People by Doing Things Together
- Post a project idea, a study group, a creative endeavour — anything related to your interests or future profession
- Students from any course can discover it and join
- Tags help you find people who share your skills and ambitions
- Working on something together is the most natural way to form genuine connections
- Collaborating on profession-related projects builds confidence and practical experience before you even graduate

### 2. Events — Shape What Happens Around You
- Any student can suggest an event they want to see happen
- Suggestions go through moderation so the best ideas get approved
- Approved events are visible to everyone — no more missing out
- This gives students agency: you're not waiting for things to happen, you're making them happen
- Shared experiences — whether a workshop, a social, or a hackathon — are how communities form

### 3. Your Activity — See Your Impact
- Track what you've created and proposed
- See the status of your event suggestions
- Watch your contributions grow over time
- Having a visible record of what you've done builds a sense of purpose and belonging

## Why It Matters

- **Mental health**: meeting people and feeling part of something directly reduces isolation and loneliness
- **Confidence**: collaborating on profession-related work makes you more prepared and self-assured about your career path
- **Belonging**: contributing to your community gives you a stake in it — you're not just attending, you're shaping it
- **Discovery**: you meet people you'd never cross paths with otherwise — different courses, different years, different perspectives

## Supporting Research

### Paper 1 — Student loneliness is a crisis

Akram, U. et al. (2025). "Prevalence and psychiatric correlates of loneliness in UK university students." *npj Mental Health Research*, 4, 8. https://doi.org/10.1038/s44184-025-00144-8

> "Moderate to severe levels of loneliness was reported by approximately two thirds of students (78.98%). Loneliness was significantly associated with each psychiatric symptom."

> "Formulating strong social bonds with other people remains a fundamental human need, providing intimacy, and belonging."

> "Reports of loneliness remain higher than those found in the UK general population. Indeed, data from the Community Life Survey found 6% of the English general population reported feeling chronically lonely" — compared to 79% of students.

> "Reducing the experience of loneliness may offer a novel preventative target in the student population."

### Paper 2 — Social activities protect wellbeing

Finnerty, R., Marshall, S. A., Imbault, C., & Trainor, L. J. (2021). "Extra-Curricular Activities and Well-Being." *Frontiers in Psychology*, 12, 647402. https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyg.2021.647402

> "Extra-curricular activities have been shown to be an important part of facilitating problem-solving, enabling expression of emotion, enhancing adaptability, and contributing to the development of interpersonal skills."

> "Activities with a social component tended to have high ratings, consistent with students attempting to replace lost social interactions."

> "The activities students were engaging in the most tended to be ones in which they incorporated a social component… extra-curricular activities were serving the important function of increasing students' social interactions with others."

> "The number of students who reported reaching out for support… was only 24%, despite 67% scoring in the high anxiety category" — showing students need proactive solutions, not just reactive ones.

> "Providing a variety of proactive social (group) therapy options for students would likely contribute substantially to students' mental health and well-being."

### Paper 3 — Collaboration builds career confidence

Naseer, F. et al. (2025). "Project based learning framework integrating industry collaboration to enhance student future readiness in higher education." *Scientific Reports*, 15, 24985. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-025-10385-4

> "A significant improvement in student employability skills, with an average increase of 25% in self-reported competencies in job-related skills."

> "Confidence in job search strategies increased by 40%."

> "81% highlighted strengthened communication, analytical, and collaborative capabilities through industry-situated project briefs."

> "Some evidence also indicates improved mental health, resilience, and well-being through the social connections built working within creative collectives."

> Student quote: "The multiple touchpoints working through ambiguous business challenges with industry veteran guidance allowed gradually improving how we frame recommendations, synthesize insights across disciplines and anticipate consequences — sharpening skills directly applicable for senior strategy roles."
